1. OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Survival Pioneer is a third-person survival action game built in Unity. The
player is a "Pioneer" exploring a forest wilderness camp environment. Core
gameplay loops:

  - Manage survival vitals (health, hunger, thirst, energy)
  - Gather resources from the world
  - Craft items at cooking pots and workbenches
  - Fight enemies with melee weapons
  - Complete quests from an NPC guide
  - Earn and spend Pi currency
  - Explore the map and use scanner/binocular tools

Main playable scene: Assets/Pioneer.unity
Core project folder: Assets/_Project/

Movement is powered by ECM2 (third-person character controller).
UI uses a Shift sci-fi theme with a journal hub for most panels.

2. CONTROLS
================================================================================

  WASD          Move
  Mouse         Look / orbit camera
  Scroll        Camera zoom (optics) / map zoom
  Space         Jump
  Shift         Sprint (uses energy)
  Ctrl          Crouch

  E             Interact (pickups, stations, NPCs, enemy loot, recipes)
  I             Open inventory tab (journal)
  J             Toggle journal hub
  M             Toggle world map
  Escape        Pause menu / close panels

  1, 2          Primary / secondary weapon hotbar slots
  3–0           General hotbar slots
  N             Scanner tool (toolbar)
  B             Binoculars tool (toolbar)

  LMB           Melee attack (hold for power hit)
  RMB           Block (one-handed weapons) / optics zoom when using tools

Weapon draw: press the same hotbar key again to draw or holster a weapon.

4. SURVIVAL & DEATH
================================================================================

FOUR VITAL STATS (SurvivalStats.cs)
  - Health
  - Hunger
  - Thirst
  - Energy

MECHANICS
  - Hunger, thirst, and energy drain passively over time
  - Sprinting drains energy
  - When hunger or thirst fall below a threshold (default 25%), health drains
  - Consumable items restore individual stats (ItemData fields)
  - Optional health regeneration (disabled by default in current setup)

DEATH
  - Player death pauses gameplay
  - Death popup with retry / end game options (UIManager)
  - Respawn restores position and vitals (PlayerDeathHandler)

6. COMBAT
================================================================================

PLAYER MELEE (MeleeCombatController.cs)
  - One-handed combo chain (5 attack animation states)
  - Two-handed attack set (4 animation states)
  - Power / critical hit: hold LMB ~2 seconds, release for heavy strike
  - Charge wind-up pose begins at ~1.5 seconds of holding
  - Block with RMB (one-handed weapons only; two-handers cannot block)
  - Weapon switch via dedicated input

HIT DETECTION
  - Primary: WeaponHitbox.cs — box collider on equipped weapon scans overlaps
    during swing window (~0.45s); hits follow weapon motion, not camera aim
  - Fallback: sphere overlap in front of player if no weapon visual exists
  - Camera raycasts removed as primary hit method (fixes orbit-camera misses)

WEAPON STATS (per ItemData)
  - Melee damage + random range
  - Critical damage multiplier
  - Attack range, cooldown
  - Gather power (can hit resource nodes with weapon)

COMBAT FEEDBACK
  - Floating damage numbers (CombatUiSpawner.cs)
  - Blood splatter VFX (CombatHitVfx.cs, FX_Blood_Splatter prefab)
  - Weapon swing and hit sounds (GameAudioManager.cs)
  - Enemy noise events alert nearby AI (EnemyNoiseEvents.cs)

TRAINING DUMMY
  - Practice target with high HP (TrainingDummy.cs)
  - Floating health bar and damage numbers (DummyCombatUI.cs)
  - Prefab: Prefabs/Combat/TrainingDummy.prefab
  - Placed in Pioneer.unity scene

7. ENEMIES & LOOT
================================================================================

ENEMY TYPES
  - Generic Enemy (placeholder capsule prefab)
  - Gongo (troll model)
  - The Evil One (humanoid enemy)
  - Definitions: Assets/_Project/Data/Enemies/*.asset (EnemyDefinition.cs)
  - Prefabs: Assets/_Project/Prefabs/Combat/

ENEMY AI (EnemyAiController.cs)
  - Behavior presets: Aggressive Hunter, Guard, Patrol Investigator, Ambush
  - Movement modes: Stationary, Idle, Wander, Patrol
  - States: Idle, Wander, Patrol, Investigate noise, Return home, Chase,
    Attack, Search for lost target
  - Senses: vision (range + FOV), hearing, proximity (EnemySenses.cs)
  - Responds to player noise from combat and movement

ENEMY COMBAT
  - Melee attacks with windup, cooldown, and damage to player SurvivalStats
  - Floating health bar above enemy (EnemyHealthBarPresenter.cs)
  - Death animation and AI/combat shutdown on death

RESPAWN
  - Enemies can respawn at spawn point after configurable delay
  - Loot phase pauses respawn until loot is taken or loot timer expires (20s)

ENEMY LOOT (EnemyLootable.cs)
  - Enabled on enemy prefabs via EnemyDefinition loot settings
  - On death: rolls Pi coins (min/max) and random items from loot pool
  - Empty loot pool falls back to item registry (excluding quest items and
    melee weapons from random rolls)
  - Press E near corpse opens loot dialog (EnemyLootDialogUI.cs):
      [Loot]      Take one reward entry
      [Loot All]  Take everything
      [Close]     Close dialog; corpse remains lootable until empty or timer
  - Respawn resumes after all loot taken or loot delay expires

EDITOR
  - Enemy Prefab Creator (Tools > Survival Pioneer > Combat)
  - Update All Enemy Prefabs And Scene (EnemyLootSetupUtility.cs)

8. WORLD INTERACTION (E KEY)
================================================================================

Central system: WorldUseController.cs

E-KEY PRIORITY ORDER
  1. Aimed priority interactables (quest giver, crafting station when aimed)
  2. Active enemy loot (corpse in range)
  3. Item pickups and recipe scroll pickups (aim-assisted reticle)
  4. Other registered IWorldUsable objects
  5. Raycast fallback for generic world use

AIM ASSIST FOR PICKUPS
  - PickupAimReticleUI.cs — screen reticle for focused pickup
  - PickupProximityDotUI.cs — dots for nearby collectibles
  - Crafting stations and quest givers take priority over nearby ground loot

INTERACTION PROMPT
  - "Press E to ..." text shown via UIManager when near interactables

9. RESOURCE GATHERING
================================================================================

RESOURCE NODES (ResourceNode.cs)
  - World objects with hit threshold (must be struck multiple times)
  - Yield items added to inventory via ResourceGatherer
  - Can be hit with melee weapons (uses weapon gather power)

RESOURCE GATHERER (ResourceGatherer.cs)
  - Attached to player; defines pickup range and gather range/layers
  - Notifies quest system when resources are collected

WORLD ITEM PICKUPS (ItemPickup.cs)
  - Physical items on ground (rocks, mushrooms, weapons, etc.)
  - Press E to collect into inventory
  - Optional respawn timer (20–90 seconds)
  - Pi-infused items grant Pi currency on pickup (ItemData.isPiInfused)

10. CRAFTING & RECIPES
================================================================================

STATIONS (CraftingStation.cs)
  - Cooking pot  — CraftingStationType.Cooking
  - Workbench    — CraftingStationType.Workbench
  - Press E while aimed at station to open journal craft tab + side craft panel
  - Scene prefabs: Prefabs/Crafting/Stations/

RECIPE SYSTEM
  - RecipeDefinition assets in Data/Crafting/Recipes/
  - RecipeRegistry (Resources/Crafting/RecipeRegistry.asset)
  - CraftingManager tracks discovered recipes and pending scrolls

RECIPE LEARNING FLOW
  1. Find a recipe scroll pickup in the world (RecipePickup prefabs)
  2. Press E to pick up scroll (goes to pending scrolls)
  3. Open journal Craft tab; learn scroll to permanently unlock recipe
  4. Craft at correct station when ingredients are in inventory

CURRENT RECIPES

  stone_salve        Workbench — 3x Rock

CRAFTING UI
  - CraftingUI.cs — recipe list, ingredient check, craft button
  - Embedded in journal Craft tab and standalone side panel at stations
  - Recipe hover tooltips show ingredients and station requirement
